Key Rules — 基本ルール
Resume Writing Rules
ペン
Use black ink only. Never use pencil or colored pens.
修正
No corrections allowed. Rewrite from scratch if you make a mistake.
写真
3×4cm, suit photo, within last 3 months. Plain background.
日付
Use the date you submit the resume, not the date you wrote it.
年号
Use Japanese era (令和) or Western year consistently throughout.

履歴書に間違いがあると印象が悪い
Mistakes on your resume leave a very bad impression. Always have someone review it before submitting. Use formal Japanese throughout.

Motivation & Self-PR — 志望動機・自己PR

自分の良さを上手にアピールしよう
Section 3 of 5 — Key Points
志望動機 Comparison
NG 悪い例
「日本で働きたいです。御社は有名なので志望しました。」
OK 良い例
「御社の○○事業に魅力を感じました。私の○○の経験を活かしたいです。」
Self-PR Structure — 自己PRの構成
① 自分の一番の強みを選ぶ
Choose your best characteristic
Pick one strength from your self-analysis (e.g., teamwork, patience, language skills)
② 具体的なエピソードを話す
Tell a specific episode that proves it
Give a real story: "At my part-time job, I solved X by doing Y"
③ 会社でどう活かせるか結ぶ
Connect it to the company
End by explaining how you can contribute to THIS specific company
志望動機のポイント
In Japan, 志望動機 (motivation for applying) is one of the most important parts. Companies want to know: Why THIS company? Research the company before writing. Generic answers like “I want to work in Japan” are not enough — be specific about what attracted you.
💡
Writing Tip
「なぜこの会社なのか」を明確に！
Make “Why this company?” crystal clear. Mention specific products, services, or values of the company that appeal to you.

本人希望記入欄 — Personal Requests Section
This section is where you write special requests. But be very careful — writing too many requests gives a bad impression. If you have no special requests, write:

「貴社の規定に従います」
(I will follow your company's regulations)
Writing Rules — 記入ルール
How to Write Requests
注意
Don't write too many requests — it gives a negative impression.
理由
If you have a request, always include the reason WHY.
空欄
Never leave it blank! Write 「貴社の規定に従います」.
NG Pattern
「特になし」
Writing “Nothing in particular”
Never write 特になし. Use 「貴社の規定に従います」 or 「貴社の規定に準じます」 instead.

Entering & Leaving — 入退室のマナー

入室から退室までの流れを覚えよう
Section 1 of 5 — Key Points
Entering — 入室の手順
1
Knock 3 Times
Knock on the door 3 times slowly. Wait for 「どうぞ」 before entering.
コンコンコン
2
Enter & Greet
Open the door, step in, turn to face the interviewer, and greet with a bow.
「失礼いたします」
3
Walk to Chair
Walk to the left side of the chair. Stand straight and introduce yourself.
「○○と申します。よろしくお願いいたします」
4
Wait for Permission
Do NOT sit until told 「お掛けください」. Then say:
「失礼いたします」
5
Sit Properly
Sit shallow on the chair. Back straight, hands on knees. Bag on the floor beside the chair.
Leaving — 退室の手順
1
Stand Up
Stand up from the chair and move to its side.
「本日はありがとうございました」
2
Bow Deeply
Bow at 45 degrees beside the chair (最敬礼).
3
Walk to Door
Walk to the door, turn around, and bow again.
「失礼いたします」
4
Exit Quietly
Open the door, step out, close it gently. Stay polite until you leave the building.
💡
Important
笑顔・大きな声・目線の3つが大切
① Smile with teeth slightly showing
② Speak in a loud, clear voice
③ Make eye contact — don't look up or down

Voice & Manner Rules
声
Speak in a loud, clear voice. Don't mumble.
笑顔
Always smile! A smile solves everything in Japanese interviews.
うなずき
Nod while listening. Show you're engaged with the conversation.
💡

Say everything with a big voice! In Japanese interviews, energy and enthusiasm are valued even more than perfect Japanese. A bright attitude shows やる気 (motivation).

Common Questions ① — よく聞かれる質問(前)

面接でよく聞かれる質問に備えよう
Section 1 of 5 — Key Points
Questions 1-6 — 質問リスト
1
将来の夢は何ですか？
What is your dream for the future?
💡
Be specific and connect it to working in Japan. Show you have a long-term vision.
2
日本には何年くらい居たいですか？
How many years do you want to stay in Japan?
💡
Companies want long-term employees. Show commitment: “I want to build my career here.”
3
学校ではどんなことを学びましたか？
What did you study at school?
💡
Mention your major and connect it to the job. If unrelated, explain what skills you gained.
4
長所と短所はどんなところですか？
What are your strengths and weaknesses?
💡
For weaknesses, always explain what you're doing to improve. Turn negatives into positives.
5
母国ではなくなぜ日本で就職を決意しましたか？
Why did you choose to work in Japan?
💡
Mention specific aspects of Japanese culture, technology, or industry that attracted you.
6
弊社を志願した理由を教えてください
Please tell us why you applied to our company.
💡
Research the company! Mention specific projects, products, or values. Never give a generic answer.
💡
Answer Tips
具体的なエピソードを入れましょう
Always include specific episodes. “I like Japan” is not enough — explain a specific moment that made you want to work here.

Common Questions ② — よく聞かれる質問(後)

残りの頻出質問に備えよう
Section 2 of 5 — Key Points
Questions 7-12 — 質問リスト
７．趣味はどんなことですか？
What are your hobbies? Connect hobbies to skills: teamwork, focus, creativity.
８．アルバイトはどんなことをしていますか？
What part-time work do you do? Highlight customer service, responsibility, teamwork.
９．日本に来て、困ったことは？
Difficulties in Japan? Show how you overcame challenges. Resilience matters.
１０．日本に来て、楽しかったことは？
Happy moments in Japan? Show appreciation for Japanese culture and people.
１１．仕事で大切にしていることは？
What do you value at work? Mention teamwork, quality, communication, learning.
１２．なにか質問はありますか？
Any questions for us? ALWAYS prepare 2-3 questions! Ask about training, team culture, etc.
Practice — 練習問題
Try Answering This!
Q
仕事をするうえで、どんなことを大切にしていますか？
✓
Think of a specific value + episode + how it helps the company.
🕐
Aim for 30-60 seconds per answer. Not too short, not too long.
NG for Question 12
「特にありません」
“Nothing in particular”
NEVER say this! Always prepare 2-3 questions. It shows genuine interest. Ask about work content, team culture, or training programs.

Post-Offer Steps — 内定後の手続き

内定をもらった後にやるべきこと
Section 3 of 5 — Key Points
Process — 内定後の流れ
1
内定通知確認
Receive and verify the offer letter. Check salary, position, start date, and conditions carefully.
▼
2
入社意思表明
Tell the company you accept. Do this quickly — within the deadline. Call or email formally.
▼
3
ビザ切替
Switch from student/designated activities visa to work visa (就労ビザ). The company will help with paperwork.
▼
4
必要書類準備
Prepare: 学歴証明書, 卒業証明書, 成績証明書, 資格合格証, パスポート, 在留カード
▼
5
入社！
Start your new career! Continue improving Japanese and learn industry-specific terms during the waiting period.
While Waiting — ビザ審査中
Preparation During Visa Processing
While your visa is being processed:
• 日本語能力の向上 — Improve your Japanese skills
• 専門用語の予習 — Study industry-specific terminology
• 会社までの行き方の確認 — Check your route to the office
• 会社概要の再確認 — Re-study the company overview
💡
Visa Tip
ビザ申請は早めに！
Start visa paperwork immediately. Processing takes 1-3 months. Your school and company will help, but YOU are responsible for gathering documents from your home country.
